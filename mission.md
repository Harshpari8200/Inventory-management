🌌 Inventory

Inventory management is a high-performance, modular Inventory Management System (IMS) designed to digitize and streamline stock-related operations. By replacing manual registers and scattered tracking methods with a centralized, real-time "Command Center," it brings order to warehouse chaos.
🚀 The Mission

To provide Inventory Managers and Warehouse Staff with a professional-grade tool that handles everything from the first vendor receipt to the final customer delivery.
🛠️ Core User Flows
1. The Manager’s Strategy

Managers oversee the high-level health of the business through a Dynamic Dashboard:

    KPI Tracking: Monitor total products, low-stock alerts, and pending transfers at a glance.

    Product Definition: Create and categorize goods with unique SKUs and Units of Measure.

    Automation: Set reordering rules to ensure the warehouse never runs dry.

2. The Staff’s Execution

Warehouse operators use the "Operations" suite to move physical goods:

    Receipts: Process incoming stock from suppliers and validate it to automatically update levels.

    Internal Transfers: Move items between locations (e.g., Rack A to Rack B) with every move logged in a permanent ledger.

    Delivery Orders: Pick and pack items for customer shipment, triggering an automatic stock decrease upon validation.

    Adjustments: Perform physical counts and fix mismatches for damaged or lost items.

🎨 Design Philosophy: Dark Mode & Minimalist

    Theme: Deep charcoal background with high-contrast accent colors (Emerald for "Ready," Amber for "Waiting").

    Interface: A tiling-window layout inspired by professional developer environments.

    Performance: Optimized for speed with smart filters and SKU-based search.

📊 Stock Lifecycle Example

    Arrive: Receive 100kg of Steel (+100).

    Move: Transfer from Main Store to Production Rack (Stock total remains same, location updates).

    Ship: Deliver 20kg to a customer (-20).

    Audit: Adjust for 3kg of damaged steel (-3).

    Ledger: Every single one of these steps is etched into the Stock Ledger for 100% accountability.
